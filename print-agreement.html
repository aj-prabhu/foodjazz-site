Coaching Agreement
Between Akshay Prabhu ("Coach") operating as Food Jazz, and ("Client"). Effective .
1. Program
Scope
A one-month (30-day) in-home culinary coaching program: one kitchen audit (~2 hours), four weekly in-person sessions (~2 hours each), one grocery shopping trip, daily cooking prompts and feedback via text and voice memo, and a post-program dinner party at which Coach attends as a guest.
Duration & Location
30 consecutive days from the agreed start date, plus the post-program dinner. All in-person sessions take place at the Client's home kitchen; the grocery trip at a mutually agreed store. Specific dates are scheduled collaboratively during the kitchen audit.
Communication
Coach responds to messages within a reasonable window during normal waking hours. Daily feedback does not imply 24/7 availability.
2. Payment
Fee
The fee is the amount stated in the Stripe payment link issued to the Client. Payment is due in full before Day 1. The 30 days do not begin until payment is received and the Client is stocked per the personalized stocking plan.
Refund Policy
If the Client is unsatisfied within the first seven (7) days of the program, Coach will issue a full refund, no questions asked. After Day 7, no refunds.
3. Food, Ingredients & Equipment
Groceries
The Client purchases and pays for all groceries, pantry items, and consumable ingredients used during the program, including ingredients Coach uses or demonstrates with during in-home sessions and the grocery trip. Coach may bring small quantities of spices or specialty ingredients for demonstration, at Coach's expense.
Equipment
Client purchases kitchen equipment per the personalized stocking plan. Coach may bring tools (wok, knives, etc.) for demonstration; these tools remain Coach's property and leave with Coach.
Food Prepared During Sessions
Food prepared during sessions is made collaboratively for instructional purposes. Both parties may consume it as part of the learning experience.
4. Liability, Safety & Insurance
Assumption of Risk
Cooking involves inherent risks: cuts, burns, hot oil splatter, slips, smoke inhalation, allergic reactions. The Client acknowledges these risks and agrees to exercise reasonable care. Coach prioritizes safety instruction but is not a substitute for the Client's own attention.
Mutual Hold Harmless
Each party holds the other harmless from any injuries, damages, or losses sustained during or as a result of the program, except in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct. This includes injuries the Client may sustain in their own kitchen and any injuries Coach may sustain while at the Client's home.

4. Liability, Safety & Insurance (cont.)
Property
Each party is responsible for their own property. Coach is not liable for incidental wear, damage, or loss to the Client's kitchen, appliances, cookware, or home arising from normal program activities. The Client is not liable for incidental damage to tools Coach brings, except in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct. The Client is responsible for maintaining homeowner's or renter's insurance covering their home and contents.
Insurance Disclosure
Coach does not currently carry commercial general liability insurance for in-home services. The Client acknowledges this; the mutual hold-harmless provisions govern the parties' allocation of risk.
Limitation of Liability
To the maximum extent permitted by law, Coach's total liability to the Client arising out of or related to this agreement is limited to the fees actually paid by the Client to Coach. Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.
Dietary Restrictions & No Medical Advice
The Client is responsible for disclosing all food allergies, intolerances, sensitivities, and dietary restrictions in writing before the program begins. Coach is not a registered dietitian, nutritionist, or medical professional. Nothing in the program constitutes medical, nutritional, or dietary advice.
Pets & Children
The Client is responsible for the safety of pets and children present in the home during sessions, including keeping them clear of hot surfaces, sharp tools, and active cooking areas.
5. Photo, Video & Testimonial Release
The Client grants Coach an irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide license to use the Client's name, likeness, voice, image, and any photos, videos, text messages, voice memos, written feedback, and other content created during or arising from the program for marketing, social media, website, advertising, editorial, and promotional purposes related to Food Jazz. The Client is not entitled to compensation beyond the program itself. Coach will not publish content revealing the Client's home address or other sensitive personal information without express consent. The Client may request, in writing, that specific content be excluded going forward; previously published content may not be retrievable.
6. IP, Confidentiality & Other Terms
Intellectual Property
The Food Jazz curriculum, methodology, written materials, prompts, frameworks, and digital or printed assets shared with the Client remain Coach's sole property. The Client receives a non-exclusive, non-transferable license for personal use. The Client agrees not to redistribute, republish, sell, or use Food Jazz materials to teach or coach others without prior written consent.
Confidentiality
Each party keeps the other's non-public personal and professional information confidential. Coach will not share photos, observations, or details of the Client's home or household beyond what is permitted under Section 5.
Scheduling & Cancellation
In-person sessions may be rescheduled with at least 24 hours notice. If Coach cancels, an equivalent makeup session is scheduled at no additional charge. If the Client cancels with less than 24 hours notice or misses a session without notice, that session counts as completed. Daily prompts and feedback continue regardless.
Independent Contractor
Coach is an independent contractor, not an employee, partner, or joint venturer of the Client.
Force Majeure
Neither party is liable for failure or delay due to causes beyond their reasonable control (illness, injury, family emergency, severe weather, fire, public-health orders, utility outages). The affected party will notify the other promptly and reschedule in good faith.
Governing Law & General
This agreement is governed by the laws of California; disputes will be resolved in state or federal courts located in Alameda County. This document, together with the Stripe payment link and personalized stocking plan, constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes prior discussions. If any provision is held unenforceable, the rest remains in effect. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
