Commitment Contract
— The Thirty-Day Practice —

A Promise to the Kitchen.

This is not a legal document. No court will enforce it, no clause is binding. It carries only the weight of having been signed, which, as it happens, is most of the weight there is.

· · ·

I, , hereby commit to the following for the next thirty days.

  1. Cook every day. Even when I'm tired and even when it's something small. Keeping the rhythm going matters more than making anything impressive.
  2. Eat the bad ones. When a meal doesn't work, I'll figure out what went wrong and try again. Bad food is part of the process, not a sign to stop.
  3. Try things I'd normally skip. Unfamiliar ingredients, techniques I've avoided, dishes I've been told are too hard. This is one of the few times I'll get to be a beginner on purpose.
  4. Be honest. If something tastes bad, I'll say so. If I miss a day, I'll be upfront about it. If I'm confused, I'll ask instead of guessing.
  5. Reward myself when it's done. Something specific I'll actually look forward to:
    (a long massage, dinner at the place I've been saving, that knife I've been eyeing — or whatever else.)
Signed in good faith and hunger for change,
The Cook
