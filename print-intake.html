Intake Interview
Client:   Date:

Have a conversation, not an interrogation. Know the questions, hit them naturally, jot notes after or voice memo yourself once you leave.

Current Relationship with Food
Walk me through a typical weekday. When do you eat, what, how do you get it?
What's your go-to when you're hungry and don't want to think?
How much are you spending on food per month?
Delivery, groceries, eating out. Rough number is fine.
Have you tried to cook more before? What happened?
Last thing you cooked that you were actually proud of?
What They Want
If this works perfectly, what does your kitchen life look like in 30 days?
Specific dishes or cuisines you want to make?
Do you cook for anyone else? Do you want to?
What would make you feel like the money was well spent?
Practical Constraints
Dietary restrictions, allergies, or strong dislikes?
How much time can you realistically spend cooking? Weeknight vs. weekend?
Kitchen situation: gas or electric? Counter space? Storage?
You'll see this during the walkthrough. Note anything they mention.
Any equipment you love using or are intimidated by?
Personality & Motivation
Are you competitive with yourself? Do streaks or challenges motivate you?
What's the reward you'd give yourself for cooking every day for 30 days?
This goes on the commitment contract.
How do you want me to push you? Gentle nudges or "you didn't cook today"?
Overall Impressions / Things to Remember
